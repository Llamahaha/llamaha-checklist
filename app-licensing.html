Licensing Basics

How product access usually works

Model

Work account access

Many products are tied to the work email account you use to sign in, especially Microsoft 365, Adobe, Autodesk, ArcGIS, and Bentley apps.

Model

Subscription or seat assignment

Some products require a specific subscription, seat, role, or extension to be assigned to your account before the app will show the right features.

Model

Product year, edition, or plan

CAD, PDF, GIS, and accounting products often depend on the exact edition, release year, or plan your team expects you to use.

Model

Company profile or organization

Some apps also depend on the correct company profile, ArcGIS organization, Bentley datasource, or workspace being selected after sign-in.

Model

Company license server or authorization file

Some products still use a license server, an authorization file, or a similar company-managed license source. The app has to be pointed at the right server or file before it opens normally.

What You May Need

Details that usually determine access

Account

The exact work email account

Use the same work account your company expects for the product. Personal accounts and alternate profiles are a common reason access looks different than expected.

Product

The app name and version or year

Know the exact product, version, edition, or release year you are expected to use. This matters most for Autodesk, Bentley, ArcGIS, Foxit, and Bluebeam.

Plan

The plan, role, or add-on you need

Microsoft plans, ArcGIS roles and extensions, and subscription tiers often determine which features appear after sign-in.

Organization

The correct company profile or portal

Some products depend on the correct Adobe company profile, ArcGIS portal, Bentley datasource, or workspace in addition to the sign-in itself.
