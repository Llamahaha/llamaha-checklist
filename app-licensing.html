Common Signs

These usually point to a licensing or activation problem

Sign

The app says trial or unlicensed

This usually means the app did not pick up the correct account, seat, or subscription after launch.

Sign

Paid features are missing

You may be signed in correctly but still using the wrong profile, plan, or product edition for that workflow.

Sign

Browser works but desktop does not

This often means the account is valid but the local app needs a sign-out, refresh, or update to catch up.

Sign

The app worked before an update or device change

Restarts, updates, account switches, and new devices can all cause licensing or activation prompts to appear again.

Try This First

Simple checks before you contact support

Step 1

Restart the app and your computer

A fresh restart can clear stale sign-in or activation prompts, especially after updates.

Step 2

Sign out, then sign back in

Use the exact work account that should have the license. Personal accounts are a common cause of missing access.

Step 3

Check for updates

If the app recently changed, install the latest approved update and reopen it before trying again.

Step 4

Try the browser or companion app

If the browser version works but the desktop app does not, that is useful information to share with support.

What To Send Support

Have these details ready if it still fails

Account

Your work email

Use the exact work account that should have access to the product. Many licensing issues come from signing in with the wrong account or profile.

Product

The app name and version

Know the product name and, if possible, the version or year shown in the app. This matters most for CAD, PDF, and accounting software.

Message

A screenshot of the error

Capture the exact message if the app says trial, unlicensed, subscription required, activation failed, or sign-in is needed.

Comparison

Browser versus desktop

Check whether the same account works in the vendor portal or browser version. That helps separate account access problems from local app problems.
